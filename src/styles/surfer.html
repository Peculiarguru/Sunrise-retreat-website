About Surfing

Surfing is a popular water sport in which the rider catches a wave and rides it on a surfboard. It originated in Polynesian culture and has since spread all over the world. Surfing is a great way to stay fit and enjoy the outdoors, and is suitable for all ages and skill levels.

Types of Waves

  • Breaking Waves

    • Breaking Waves are dynamic, mesmerizing, and awe-inspiring natural phenomena. They are a symbol of the raw power and beauty of the ocean. With their towering crests and crashing energy, Breaking Waves provide an irresistible lure for surfers and beachgoers alike. Whether you're watching from the shore or riding the waves, Breaking Waves are a breathtaking experience that will leave you in awe of the natural world and the infinite possibilities it holds.

  • Groundswells

    • Groundswells are a type of ocean wave that are known for their powerful, rolling swell and long distances. Groundswells are created by distant storms and can travel thousands of miles across the open ocean before reaching the shore. With their steady, building energy and consistent size, groundswells are a favorite among surfers for their challenging and exhilarating ride. These waves offer an unforgettable experience, as surfers carve down their faces and feel the rush of gliding across the water. Whether you're an experienced surfer or a beach-goer watching from the shore, groundswells are a stunning display of the ocean's raw power and beauty.

  • Windswells

    • Windswells are a type of ocean wave created by the wind blowing over the surface of the water. These waves are known for their choppy, fast-moving appearance and can be found in many different surf conditions. Windswells are often smaller in size compared to groundswells, but they can still provide fun and exciting rides for surfers. These waves are characterized by their quick, playful energy and offer a unique challenge for surfers looking to test their skills. Whether you're a seasoned surfer or just watching from the shore, windswells are a thrilling and dynamic display of the ocean's power and beauty.

Surfboards

  • Shortboards

    • Shortboards range in length from 5' to 7'. They have nose rocker (an upturned nose) to help you avoid burying the tip of your board under water (pearling), and possess two to four fins that allow for quick, radical turns.

  • Longboards

    • Longboards are best suitable for a long surfing session without getting too tired, especially if one doesn't have so much experience catching and paddling for waves, because it does not require such strength to catch the wave or paddle back to the peak, as with a shortboard.
